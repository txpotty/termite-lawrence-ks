Common Termite Problems and How We Can Help

Recognizing the signs of a termite infestation early is critical to minimizing damage. Many property owners in Olathe, KS, may not even realize they have a termite problem until significant damage has occurred. Here are some common issues that indicate you may need professional termite control, and how Lawrence Termite Control can provide the solution.

Mud Tubes on Walls or Foundations

Mud tubes, often found on exterior walls, foundations, or even interior surfaces, are a clear sign of subterranean termites. These tubes are made of soil, wood, and termite saliva and serve as protected pathways for termites to travel between their underground colonies and their food source (your home). Discovering mud tubes is a strong indicator of active termite activity and requires immediate professional attention.

If you see mud tubes, do not disturb them immediately. Call Lawrence Termite Control. Our experts can confirm it is termite activity and determine the extent of the infestation before implementing a targeted treatment plan that eliminates the colony responsible for the mud tubes and prevents further foraging.

Discarded Termite Wings

Swarming termites, also known as reproductives, emerge from mature colonies to establish new ones. After swarming, they shed their wings. Finding piles of small, translucent wings near windowsills, doors, or light sources is a common sign of a termite swarm and indicates a nearby mature colony. This is a significant warning sign that your property is at risk of infestation or already infested.

The presence of discarded wings means there's a termite colony actively trying to expand. Lawrence Termite Control will conduct a thorough inspection to locate the source of the swarm and implement a comprehensive treatment to eliminate the existing colony and protect your property from new infestations.

Damaged Wood Structures

Termites feed on cellulose, the main component of wood. As they consume wood, they leave behind hollowed-out sections or create tunnels within the structure. You might notice signs of damaged wood, such as:

  • Wood that sounds hollow when tapped.
  • Darkening or blistering of wood surfaces.
  • Sagging floors or ceilings.
  • Windows or doors that stick.

These are all potential indicators of termite damage. Ignoring these signs can lead to significant structural compromise over time, requiring expensive repairs.

If you suspect termite damage, Lawrence Termite Control will assess the extent of the damage and confirm if it is indeed termite-related. We will then develop a treatment plan to eliminate the termites causing the damage and provide guidance on necessary repairs to restore the structural integrity of your property.

Frass (Termite Droppings)

Drywood termites, unlike subterranean termites, live entirely within the wood they infest and do not require contact with the soil. They create kick-out holes in the wood and push out their fecal pellets, which are small, hard, and often resemble sawdust. This material is called frass. Finding piles of frass near wooden furniture, structural beams, or window frames can indicate a drywood termite infestation.

Finding frass is a clear sign of drywood termite activity. Lawrence Termite Control has specialized treatments for drywood termites, often involving localized treatments or fumigation depending on the scope of the infestation. We will accurately identify the type of termite and apply the most effective treatment to eliminate the colony and prevent further damage.

Clicking Sounds Within Walls

While it may seem unusual, you might occasionally hear faint clicking or rustling sounds coming from within your walls, particularly at night when the house is quiet. In some cases, this can be the sound of termites chewing on the wood. While other pests can also cause noises in walls, if you suspect termites, it is worth investigating.

If you hear unexplained noises that you suspect could be termites, Lawrence Termite Control can perform an inspection using specialized equipment to detect termite activity within walls and other inaccessible areas. This allows us to confirm the presence of termites and accurately target our treatment.

Tight-Fitting Doors and Windows

Termites can introduce moisture into the wood as they feed, causing it to swell and warp. This can lead to doors and windows becoming difficult to open or close. While this can also be caused by humidity, if combined with other potential termite signs, it warrants investigation.

If you experience consistently sticking doors or windows and suspect termites, Lawrence Termite Control will inspect the surrounding woodwork for signs of infestation. We will identify if termites are the cause of the warping and implement treatment to stop their activity and prevent further moisture damage to the wood.

Blistered or Peeling Paint

Termite activity can cause bubbling or blistering of paint or wallpaper as they tunnel beneath the surface and introduce moisture. This is often mistaken for water damage, but upon closer inspection, it might reveal termite galleries underneath.

If you notice unusual blistering or peeling paint, especially in areas with wood structures, Lawrence Termite Control can investigate to determine the cause. If termites are responsible, we will implement a targeted treatment to eliminate the infestation and provide advice on repairing the cosmetic damage.

Frequently Asked Questions

We understand you likely have questions about termite control. Here are some frequently asked questions our clients in Olathe, KS, often ask, along with our expert answers.

How long does termite treatment take?

The duration of termite treatment varies depending on the severity of the infestation, the size of your property, and the treatment method used. A typical treatment can take several hours, and in some cases, it may require multiple visits or the use of baiting systems that take time to work effectively. Our technicians will provide you with an estimated timeline during your initial consultation and inspection.

Is termite treatment safe for my family and pets?

Yes, the safety of your family and pets is our top priority. We use products and application methods that are both highly effective against termites and designed to minimize exposure risks to non-target organisms. We will discuss any necessary precautions with you before and during the treatment process, such as temporarily vacating the premises if required for certain types of applications.

How much does termite control cost?

The cost of termite control varies depending on several factors, including the size and type of your property, the extent of the infestation, the type of termites, and the chosen treatment method. We provide a detailed and transparent estimate after a thorough inspection of your property. We believe in providing cost-effective solutions that deliver lasting protection.

What warranty or guarantee do you offer?

We stand behind the quality of our work and offer service guarantees. The specifics of the warranty may vary depending on the treatment plan, but we are committed to resolving any recurring termite issues that may arise within the warranty period. We aim for long-term solutions and customer satisfaction.

How can I prevent future termite infestations?

Preventing future termite infestations involves a combination of professional services and steps you can take as a property owner. This includes minimizing moisture around your foundation, removing wood-to-ground contact, sealing cracks in your foundation, and regularly inspecting for signs of termites. Our technicians can provide specific recommendations during your service visit.

Regular maintenance and addressing potential risk factors are key to long-term termite prevention. We can help you identify these areas and implement preventative measures.

What are the different types of termites in Olathe, KS?

The most common type of termite in Olathe, KS, is the subterranean termite. These termites live in underground colonies and build mud tubes to travel above ground to access food sources in your home's wood. Understanding the specific species is important for effective treatment, and our experts are knowledgeable in identifying all types of termites found in the region.

While less common, drywood termites and dampwood termites can also be found in certain situations. Each type has unique behaviors and requires specialized control methods. Our comprehensive inspections will accurately identify the species present.

Can I treat termites myself?

DIY termite treatments are generally not recommended. Termite colonies can be vast and complex, extending deep within structures or underground. Without the proper knowledge, tools, and products, you may only treat the visible signs of infestation, leaving the main colony intact, which will continue to cause damage. Professional termite control is the most effective way to eliminate an infestation and protect your property in the long run.

Professional exterminators have access to powerful, regulated products and specialized techniques that are not available to the public. They also have the expertise to accurately identify the type of termite and the extent of the infestation, which is crucial for effective eradication.

How often should I get a termite inspection?

We recommend getting a professional termite inspection at least once a year, even if you haven't seen any signs of termites. Termites can work silently and cause significant damage without readily visible signs. Regular inspections can detect infestations early, minimizing the cost and extent of potential damage. If you live in an area with a high risk of termite activity or have had a previous infestation, more frequent inspections may be advisable.

Preventative inspections are a worthwhile investment to protect your property from the costly damage that termites can cause. Early detection is key to effective and less expensive treatment.
